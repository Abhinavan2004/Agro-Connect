RICE

  • Field Preparation: Choose fertile soil, level the field, and enrich it with organic matter.
  • Seed Selection: Soak seeds, pre-germinate, and transplant seedlings.
  • Water Management: Keep the field flooded, especially early on, and ensure proper drainage.
  • Fertilization: Apply fertilizers at different growth stages.
  • Weed Control: Keep the field weed-free through manual or mechanical methods.
  • Pest and Disease Management: Monitor and use integrated pest management techniques.
  • Harvesting: Harvest when grains turn golden brown and dry properly.
  • Post-Harvest Handling: Store in a dry area, process, and store properly.

WHEAT

  • Field Preparation: Choose well-drained soil, plow deeply, and remove weeds.
  • Seed Selection: Choose high-quality seeds suitable for your region.
  • Planting: Sow seeds at the right depth and spacing, ensuring good soil contact.
  • Fertilization: Apply nitrogen-based fertilizer at planting and during growth stages.
  • Water Management: Ensure adequate moisture, especially during critical growth stages.
  • Weed Control: Keep the field weed-free using manual or mechanical methods.
  • Disease and Pest Management: Monitor for diseases and pests, and apply appropriate controls.
  • Harvesting: Harvest when kernels are fully developed and dry, typically in summer.
  • Threshing and Winnowing: Separate grain from the chaff using threshing and winnowing methods.

BAJRA

  • Soil Preparation: Choose well-drained sandy loam soil with good organic matter.
  • Seed Selection: Choose high-quality seeds adapted to your region's climate.
  • Planting: Sow seeds at the right depth and spacing, typically 2-3 cm deep and 25-30 cm apart.
  • Fertilization: Apply organic or balanced fertilizers based on soil test results.
  • Watering: Provide regular and adequate irrigation, especially during flowering and grain formation.
  • Weed Management: Keep the field weed-free using manual or herbicidal methods.
  • Pest and Disease Control: Monitor for pests and diseases, and apply appropriate control measures.
  • Harvesting: Harvest when grains are fully matured and dry, usually about 80-90 days after sowing.
  • Threshing: Separate grains from the panicles using threshing methods.
  • Winnowing: Remove chaff and other impurities from the grains using winnowing techniques.

GREEN PEAS

  • Site Selection: Choose a location with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
  • Seed Selection: Choose high-quality pea seeds suitable for your climate and soil conditions.
  • Planting: Sow seeds directly into the ground in early spring, about 1 inch deep and 2-3 inches apart.
  • Support: Provide support for the pea plants to climb as they grow, using trellises or stakes.
  • Watering: Keep the soil consistently moist, especially during flowering and pod formation.
  • Fertilization: Apply a balanced fertilizer or compost before planting and as needed during the growing season.
  • Weed Control: Keep the planting area free from weeds to reduce competition for nutrients and water.
  • Pest Management: Monitor for pests such as aphids and caterpillars, and use organic or chemical control methods as needed.
  • Harvesting: Harvest peas when the pods are plump and bright green, typically 60-70 days after planting.
  • Storage: Store harvested peas in the refrigerator or blanch and freeze them for long-term storage.
